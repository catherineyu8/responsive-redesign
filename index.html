Original Design

Despite containing relatively simple content, the Toronto Cupcake website is extremely frustrating to navigate through and has visually inconsistent, cluttered pages. Its poor design and lack of clear organization makes it difficult for users to quickly find the information they need, potentially driving away customers who might otherwise place an order.

I chose to specifically redesign the FAQs page because, despite its simple and straightforward purpose, its layout makes it hard for users to efficiently find the information they need.


Identifying Problems

I analyzed the usability of the FAQs page according to several different criteria:

  • Efficiency - is it fast to do typical tasks? (for frequent users)
  • Learnability - is it easy to learn? (for new users)
  • Memorability - is it easy to remember what to do? (for casual users)
  • Conceptual model - how does the interface reveal to the user how it works? (using visual/physical/audio cues)

Accessibility

Not only is the Toronto Cupcake website poorly designed in terms of efficacy, but it also raises concerns about accessibility. I used the WebAIM WAVE tool to evaluate the accessibility of the FAQs page, and it found 19 “very low contrast” errors in the dropdown and footer menus. The colors used for these menus is a dark gray and medium pink, which is not only an aesthetically poor choice in color scheme, but the contrast between colors is also hard to distinguish, especially for colorblind users. Luckily, the Toronto Cupcake website provides a high contrast mode that increases the contrast between colors on the page, however the problem remains that the colors chosen are not visually appealing, especially on high contrast mode when the color scheme becomes somewhat inconsistent.

Redesigning the Webpage

Design Choices

Main changes I made in my redesign (from the original webpage) and their impact on usability include:

  • Reformat FAQs in a single column, grouped by section headings — allows users to efficiently read straight down the page to find what they are looking for
  • Added an expandable carrot icon that flips when expanded/hidden — shows users that there is a button that can be clicked to show hidden information
  • Pinned navbar — allows users to always have easy access the main navigation tabs

I also made the following adjustments to my redesign after feedback from a peer critique

  • Removed 'faqs' tab from navbar
  • Removed 'about' tab from footer, switched order of footer tabs

Takeaways

One of my biggest takeaways from redesigning the Toronto Cupcake FAQs page was the importance of clear organization and hierarchy in design. The original page's layout made it difficult to navigate, so restructuring the FAQ content into a single-column format with distinct section headings significantly improved readability.

Additionally, I learned to both create responsive, reusable components in Figma, and implement these using HTML and CSS. Designing the components that automatically adapted formatting across different screen sizes largely improved the reusability of my design, allowing me to spend less time formatting for individual screens.

Overall, this redesign project gave me insight into the process of analyzing website usability and redesigning webpages to better enhance functionality and user experience.
